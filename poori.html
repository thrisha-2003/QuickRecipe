Poori Bhaji

Incredients

Poori (Fried Bread) Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • Water (as needed)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Bhaji (Potato Curry) Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 inch ginger, grated
  • 1/2 cup green peas (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Directions

    For Poori:

    1. Prepare the Dough:
      • In a large bowl, mix whole wheat flour, salt, and water to form a smooth and firm dough.
      • Add a few drops of oil and knead again. Allow the dough to rest for 15-20 minutes.
    2. Divide and Roll:
      • Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
      • Roll each ball into a small disc (poori) using a rolling pin.
    3. Heat Oil:
      • Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
    4. Fry the Pooris:
      • Gently slide each rolled poori into the hot oil. Press it lightly with a slotted spoon to help it puff up.
      • Fry until both sides are golden brown. Remove and place them on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.

    For Bhaji:

    1. Prepare the Base:
      • Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
      • Add asafoetida, chopped onions, green chilies, and grated ginger. Saut√© until the onions are golden brown.
    2. Add Spices:
      • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well.
    3. Add Potatoes and Peas:
      • Add the boiled and mashed potatoes. Stir to coat the potatoes with the spices.
      • If using peas, add them at this stage.
    4. Season and Cook:
      • Season with salt and cook the mixture for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
    5. Garnish and Serve:
      • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
      • Serve the hot Pooris with the flavorful Bhaji.
