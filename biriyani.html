BIRIYANI

Incredients
  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 tablespoons biryani masala
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 green cardamom pods, 4 cloves, 2-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)
  • Ghee for drizzling (optional)

Directions
  1. Step 1: Marinate the Chicken
    • In a bowl, mix chicken pieces with yogurt, biryani masala, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, chopped mint leaves, chopped coriander leaves, and salt.
    • Cover and let it marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably longer.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Rice
    • Boil soaked basmati rice until it is 70-80% cooked. Drain the water and set aside.
  3. Step 3: Sauté Onions and Tomatoes
    • In a large pan, heat oil or ghee. Add the whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon) and sauté until fragrant.
    • Add thinly sliced onions and cook until golden brown.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and the oil starts to separate.
  4. Step 4: Cook the Marinated Chicken
    • Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through.
  5. Step 5: Layering and Dum Cooking
    • In a heavy-bottomed pan or biryani pot, layer half of the partially cooked rice over the cooked chicken.
    • Sprinkle some biryani masala and drizzle ghee over the rice.
    • Add the remaining rice to form the top layer.
    • Top with fried onions (optional), biryani masala, and ghee.
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes. This is known as the "dum" cooking process.
  6. Step 6: Serve
    • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, ensuring not to break the rice grains.
    • Serve hot, garnished with additional fried onions and accompanied by raita or a side salad.
