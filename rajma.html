Rajma Chawal

Incredients

For Rajma (Red Kidney Beans):

  • 1 cup rajma (red kidney beans), soaked overnight
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 2 tablespoons oil

For Chawal (Steamed Rice):

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • Water for soaking and cooking
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (optional)

Directions

    For Rajma:

  1. Step 1: Cook the Rajma
    • Drain the soaked rajma and pressure cook with enough water, salt, and a bay leaf until they are soft and cooked. It may take around 15-20 minutes on medium heat.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Gravy
    • In a pan, heat oil. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Step 3: Add Spices
    • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cook for a minute.
    • Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  4. Step 4: Combine with Rajma
    • Add the cooked rajma along with its water to the masala.
    • Season with salt and garam masala. Simmer for 15-20 minutes on low heat to let the flavors meld.
  5. Step 5: Garnish and Serve
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Your Rajma is ready to be served with rice.

For Chawal:

  1. Step 1: Rinse and Soak
    • Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Step 2: Cook the Rice
    • In a pot, bring water to a boil.
    • Add soaked and drained rice to the boiling water.
    • Add salt and cook until the rice is tender yet firm. This usually takes about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Step 3: Drain and Serve
    • Once the rice is cooked, drain any excess water.
    • Optionally, mix in ghee for added flavor.
