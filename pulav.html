Pulav

Incredients
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, corn, etc.), chopped
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cashews or almonds, chopped
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. Step 1:

    Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.

  2. Step 2:

    In a large pan, heat ghee or oil. Add cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, and bay leaf. Sauté until fragrant.

  3. Step 3:

    Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.

  4. Step 4:

    Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.

  5. Step 5:

    Add mixed vegetables and chopped nuts. Sauté for 3-4 minutes.

  6. Step 6:

    Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pan. Stir gently to coat the rice with the vegetables.

  7. Step 7:

    Add turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.

  8. Step 8:

    Pour in water and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover the pan, and simmer until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.

  9. Step 9:

    Fluff the rice with a fork. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

  10. Step 10:

    Vegetable Pulav is ready to be served. Enjoy it hot with raita or yogurt!
