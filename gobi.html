GOBI MANCHURIAN

Incredients
  • 1 medium-sized cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For the sauce:
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped ginger
  • 1 cup finely chopped spring onions
  • 1/2 cup capsicum, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon cornflour mixed with 2 tablespoons water (for slurry)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

Directions
  1. Step 1: Prepare the Cauliflower
    • Blanch the cauliflower florets in hot water for 5 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Batter
    • In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, chili sauce, vinegar, and salt. Add water gradually to make a thick batter.
    • Dip each cauliflower floret into the batter, ensuring it is well coated.
    • Deep fry the battered cauliflower until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  3. Step 3: Prepare the Sauce
    • In a pan, heat oil and saut√© chopped garlic and ginger until aromatic.
    • Add spring onions and capsicum. Stir-fry for a few minutes.
    • Add soy sauce, chili sauce, tomato ketchup, sugar, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
    • Pour in water and bring the mixture to a boil.
    • Stir in the cornflour slurry to thicken the sauce. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  4. Step 4: Combine and Garnish
    • Add the fried cauliflower to the sauce. Toss until the cauliflower is well coated.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and sesame seeds (optional).
  5. Step 5: Serve
    • Serve Gobi Manchurian hot as an appetizer or with steamed rice/noodles.
