PAV BHAJI

Incredients
  • 3 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup cauliflower, finely chopped
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1 cup carrots, finely chopped
  • 1 cup bell peppers, finely chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon pav bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Pav (bread rolls) for serving

Directions

  1. Step 1:

    Boil and mash the potatoes.

  2. Step 2:

    Finely chop cauliflower, carrots, bell peppers, onions, and tomatoes.

  3. Step 3:

    In a large pan, heat butter and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Saut√© until fragrant.

  4. Step 4:

    Add finely chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.

  5. Step 5:

    Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.

  6. Step 6:

    Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil starts to separate.

  7. Step 7:

    Add mashed potatoes, finely chopped cauliflower, green peas, carrots, and bell peppers. Mix everything thoroughly.

  8. Step 8:

    Add a little water and mash the vegetables with a potato masher. Cook for 15-20 minutes on medium heat, stirring occasionally.

  9. Step 9:

    Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preferences. If needed, add more pav bhaji masala or salt.

  10. Step 10:

    Once the pav bhaji reaches the desired consistency, garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

  11. Step 11:

    Serve hot with buttered pav (bread rolls).
